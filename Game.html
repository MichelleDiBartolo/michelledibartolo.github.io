Losing focus on a mundane task? Try making a game of it.

In basic attention research studies, participants are often asked to do repetative tasks over and over again, for extended periods of time. It's not uncommon for participants to grumble about boredom or become exasperated when notified there's more of the task to complete. With concerns that this could impact their behavior, we started asking participants what they were doing during the task.

What kind of responses did we get?...
"I counted or sang a song in my head to keep me awake."

What's the problem? If some participants are engaging strategies to distract themselves, this could introduce noise to an otherwise experimentally controlled environment.

What can we do? We tested a group of participants twice, once with a basic task and once with a game version that had added sounds levels to progress through.

The results are in: Adding game features boosts engagement and motivation ratings from neutral to positive.
