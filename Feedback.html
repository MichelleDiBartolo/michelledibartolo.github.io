Feedback helps us stay on track.

Setting a long-term goal is easy. Acting on it, however, can be a challenge. Just ask the 81% of New Years Resolution setters who missed the mark on their goals at a follow-up check-in1.

What's the problem? Short-term gratification opportunities that conflict with long-term goals come up all the time. It's hard to say no to that free doughnut in your work meeting, even if you just set a new diet goal.

Why do the short-term priorities tend to win out? One reason is feedback. The effect of indulging on tasty treats is tangible. The effect of one doughnut on your overall health is not.

What can we do? Luckily, new technologies can now help make positive and negative progress toward long-term goals more tangible. In the current research project, participants played a game with a long-term goal. Throughout the game there were also goal-contradictory short-term gratification opportunities. One group received on-screen feedback about the accumulating effects of goal-contradicting behaviors. The other group just played the game, without any feedback.

The results are in: On-screen feedback reduced goal-contradictory behaviors by over 20%.
